Background:
13
Three-dimensional (3D) facial images are routinely used to measure soft tissue
14
characteristics in medical settings and biological research to accurately and
15
comprehensively quantify facial morphology (1, 2). In medicine, the use of 3D
16
17

18

19
In genetic and developmental studies of facial morphology, using large datasets, 3D
20
21

22
morphological shape variation. This technology has enabled deep phenotyping,
23
which allows for studyingone to study the genomic architecture of facial shape (4,
24
5). Provided the level of accuracy is acceptable, the objectivity, consistency, and

25

26
27
when dealing with large data sets.
28
Well-defined anatomical landmarks over the face allow the shapes to be
29
30

31
the basis of subsequent statistical analysis. Accurate placement of these landmarks
32

34

35
36

37
accurate automated 3D facial landmarking tools are highly desirable.

Various automated techniques for detecting anatomical facial landmarks have been
39
40

41
automated 3D facial landmark technique performance compared to the manual
42
method in medical and biological settings.
43
44
The purpose of this systematic review is to examine the current evidence regarding
45
the accuracy and reliability of various 3D facial automated landmarking methods
46
used in medical or and biological studies and evaluate their performance against the
